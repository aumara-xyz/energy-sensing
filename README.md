# Energy Sensing

This repository preserves a timestamped public research package for the Energy Sensing program centered on **bioenergetic control-state failure in chronic disease**.

## Status

Research synthesis and falsifiable translational program. Not treatment guidance. Not a clinical recommendation. Not proof that any single pathway or drug class explains chronic disease.

## Core Thesis

Many chronic diseases may be better understood through a measurable control-state problem:

- adaptive energy-sensing flexibility,
- structural reserve,
- and tissue target engagement.

The strongest version of the claim is not that one drug treats everything. It is that response to bioenergetic intervention may be predictable by control-state abnormality, remaining structural reserve, and verified target engagement.

## Current Strongest Claims

1. Peripheral cardiometabolic, kidney, and MASH outcome data make energy-sensing biology a legitimate medical research lane.
2. The right measurable object is control-state flexibility, not a slogan like "low AMPK" or "high mTOR."
3. Structural reserve and tissue target engagement are hard gates for translation, especially in neurologic disease.
4. Weight and glycemia null explanations must beat the framework by default unless a study separates them cleanly.
5. The shortest high-leverage falsification path is a dynamic challenge assay for AMPK/mTOR/ULK1/autophagy control-state behavior.

## Primary Falsification Paths

1. Disease tissues do not show impaired energy-sensing flexibility under controlled challenge.
2. Structural reserve fails to predict response better than diagnosis alone.
3. Target engagement does not track response in the relevant tissue.
4. Weight-loss and glycemic mediation fully explain apparent benefits in well-controlled studies.
5. Stage-enriched, target-engaged studies still fail without leaving a residual control-state signal.
